## Implications for Random Effects Structure

### Why Crossed Random Effects are Appropriate

1. **Genuine overlap exists:** 12 MPAs (52%) are sampled by multiple sources, with up to 3 sources per MPA.

2. **Same taxa measured by different sources at same locations:** For example, at South Point SMR:
   - S. purpuratus measured by both PISCO and KFM
   - M. franciscanus measured by both PISCO and KFM
   - M. pyrifera measured by PISCO, KFM, and Landsat
   - P. interruptus measured by both PISCO and KFM

3. **Independence of source effects:** The crossed structure properly accounts for the fact that source-specific methodological variation (e.g., different sampling protocols, observers, transect sizes) affects estimates independently of location-specific variation.

### Limitations of the Crossing

1. **Incomplete crossing:** Not all MPAs appear in all sources (would require 23 x 4 = 92 MPA-Source combinations; we have 41).

2. **Source-specific geographic focus:**
   - KFM focuses exclusively on Channel Islands
   - LTER focuses only on mainland sites (Naples, Campus Point)
   - This creates partial confounding between source and geography

3. **Unbalanced design:** PISCO contributes 67% of effect sizes (95/141), while LTER contributes only 6% (9/141).

### Recommended Model Specification

Given the partial crossing, the current specification with crossed random effects for MPA and Source is appropriate:

```r
rma.mv(yi = Effect_Size,
       V = SE^2,
       random = ~ 1 | MPA + ~ 1 | Source,  # Crossed random effects
       data = effect_data)
```

However, reviewers should note:
- The crossed structure captures real variation but is incomplete
- Source-geography confounding (KFM=Islands, LTER=Mainland) may affect interpretation
- Sensitivity analyses could compare nested vs crossed specifications

## Data Quality Notes

1. **Consistent MPA naming:** MPA names match across sources (e.g., "South Point SMR" in both PISCO and KFM data).

2. **Multiple response types:** Effect sizes span both density (Den) and biomass (Bio) responses, which are analyzed separately by taxa.

3. **Implementation years vary:** Some MPAs were established in 2003 (Channel Islands), others in 2012 (mainland and remaining sites).
